01 / The reluctant portfolio

A page about me? Gross. Also, apparently useful.

I fought off the idea of making a portfolio website for years because, quite honestly, the idea always felt a little too self-indulgent for my taste. A page entirely dedicated to me and what I’m doing…? Kinda made me want to throw up in my mouth.

However, this recently changed when it was pointed out to me that I’m a huge proponent of what my generation would term “side quests.”

side quest

A self-directed detour pursued because curiosity tugged first and the map showed up later.

And, come to think of it, my entire life feels like a collection of side quests. Honestly? That’s how I think it should be.

06 / People, people, people

My biggest thing is people.

Perhaps the biggest aspect of who I am as a person is my profound love for people. To say that I love people would be like saying Guy Fieri has a “passing interest” in putting sunglasses on the back of his head. It’s technically a sentence, but it doesn’t even begin to address the spiritual magnitude of what’s happening.

It’s been the greatest gift of my life to have been touched so deeply by the kindness, grace, and generosity of others in my darkest moments, and it has since become my life’s mission to inspire a sense of self-belief and emanating love for others in as many souls as I can until the day I die, in whichever ways I get the opportunity.

I truly am convicted by the belief that everyone has something great to offer, and that it is up to us as human beings to see it that way, no matter how difficult.

07 / Loose Ties

Rebuilding the places where people find each other.

Recently, it’s been my passion project to create an organization focused on gaining back what my generation has lost in the wake of the recent technological renaissance: true, authentic community.

After college, where does everyone go? This, I know, is a question that every young adult now finds themselves asking. If you’ve heard of third-space theory, some theorize that as our technology has become more enabling to us, we have lost the inconveniences that used to provide opportunities for the casual social interactions that quietly upheld our wellbeing.

Now, instead of going out and eating at a restaurant, we DoorDash it. Instead of going into the grocery store to pick the items that would line our pantries for the rest of the week, we place an order on an iOS app ahead of time and have someone else load our trunks so that we never even have to get out of the car.

Instead of hanging out at the local park after work, we stay inside and fill our evenings with artificial light and dying hopes that we’ll one day be happy when the next season of insert trash television show here comes out, knowing deep down that that’ll never be the case, but not quite being able to put our finger on why we’re so unhappy.

So I, along with a short list of incredible and ever-expanding friends of mine, am reinventing third-space interactions in a way they have never existed before, using the technology that originally brought about their demise. A place where everyone can be authentically themselves, maybe for the first time in their lives.
